## Notes on the process

- No subagent output was used verbatim without at least a targeted spot-check; the two highest-stakes numeric claims (Kalshi's fee formula, Polymarket's US-access status) were re-verified directly by me, not just re-read from the subagent's report, because they're load-bearing for the cost model and the execution-venue decision respectively.
- The two Opus reviews were given file paths to the actual drafted documents and a brief describing what to look for, explicitly withholding the design rationale behind those documents, per the "independent judgment, not agreement" instruction. Both came back with real, non-overlapping findings (one converged on the same `nonce`-specification gap from a different angle, which is a useful independent-confirmation signal, not redundant).
- Design work itself (strategy economics, phase-gate criteria, the fading-parameter formulas, all document synthesis, and every fix applied after the Opus reviews) was done directly, not delegated — consistent with the policy that ownership of money- and safety-critical decisions isn't delegated.
